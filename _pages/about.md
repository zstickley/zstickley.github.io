Thank you for stopping by and checking out my work! Let me tell you a bit about myself. I received my Ph.D. in Educaitonal Psychology in 2022 from Texas Tech University while studying in the Educational Psychology: Research Methods, Evaluation, Measurement and Statistics (REMS) program in the College of Education under Dr. Todd D. Little.

I received my Bachelors of Science degree in Psychology from Tarleton State University and have participated in a two-year research internship at the VISN 17 Center of Excellence for Research on Returning War Veterans located at the Doris Miller VA Medical Center in Waco, Texas. I am currently working as a Senior Research Analyst for the University of Georgia's Owens Institute for Behavioral Research, and have worked a quantitative consultant for Yhat Enterprises, LLC., and as a coordinator and instructor for the Stats Camp Foundation. Most of my work involves imputation of missing data and longitudinal latent variable modeling, but I also help researchers design and administer surveys in Qualtrics, particularly when it comes to implementing a planned missing data design. I also do a fair amount of methodological consulting to graduate students and post-graduate researchers around the world!

My current research interests include latent variable modeling, survey design including planned missing data designs, and modern missing data treatments. I also enjoy collaborating with other researchers and helping them tell their stories with their data!
